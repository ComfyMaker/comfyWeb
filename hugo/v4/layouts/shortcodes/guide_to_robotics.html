A robot could be anything from a remote-controlled car, a voice-activated AI chatbot, to a portable camera. If it possesses hardware components and performs a useful function, it qualifies as a robot!

Despite the diversity in robotics, all robots share some fundamental attributes. Understanding these basic aspects is not only simple but also beneficial for anyone with a creative spirit.

Brain - The Control Center

Every robot is equipped with a compact computer known as a Raspberry Pi, acting as the robot's brain.

The Raspberry Pi is a credit-card-sized, affordable, and highly versatile computer. It's the perfect choice for various robotic projects. The Raspberry Pi comes with Wi-Fi functionality, enabling you to remotely control your robot using the ComfySpace app. It also features I/O pins (input/output) to control sensors, motors and more.

Limbs - Driving Actions

Quite often, robots are designed in our own image. Most robots, therefore, perform physical actions such as moving around, emitting sounds or lights, etc.

To drive these actions, your robot might require one or more (or none at all) of these components, depending on the desired function.

These ’limbs’ can work independently or in conjunction to perform any desired action.
